My College Experience

California State University, Sacramento

I first went to Sacstate without have a major subject in mind and spent about a year and a half lost not knowing what direction I wanted to head in. After, a few general education classes some were fruitless and others does not fit the tools I had. Thus, I decided that Biology was the direction that made the most sense for me. The lower division Biology classes was exactly what I expected, difficult and mind numbingly boring. Although, Biology was difficult and boring, I kept heading towards it becasue there was no other subject that I thought was more diverse.

It was not until the third upper division Biology class known as BIO 139 Microbiology that I took intrest in Biology major. This was because, this was the first class I have ever failed in and I had to retake it the next semester. It bothered me that I had to retake a course that I dred. I told myself that I was not going fail this same course again, so I started putting more hours into studying for just this course but that did not make it easier. I was barely able to make the cut. After completing this course, I realized that all other upper division Biology course were that course with more emphasis of a topic. Equiped with this knowledge it finally made sense to me that this is what I wanted to major in.

Once, I decieded this, college started changing. It went from a place that I went to if I wanted to waste time to, a place that I needed be at because there was not enough time. due to the feeling of time shortage I started going to school seven days a week. I am glad I did because I graduated as Class of 2018 with a Bachelor of Science in Microbiology and a Minor in Chemistry.
